\section*{Low dimensional manifold reguralization}

\subsection*{Problem position}

The goal is to adjust a parametric distributed input-output computation 
\eqline{{\bf o}_n = {\bf f}_{\bf w}({\bf i}_n),}
mapping an input ${\bf i}_n \in {\cal R}^I$ onto an output ${\bf o}_n \in {\cal R}^O$, as a function of a parameter ${\bf w} \in {\cal R}^W$.

Given $N$ input ${\bf i} = \{ \cdots {\bf i}_n \cdots \}$ and $N$ corresponding loss functions $l_n(\cdot)$ we consider the empirical, i.e., average loss:
\eqline{{\cal L}({\bf w}) = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=1}^N l_n({\bf f}_{\bf w}({\bf i}_n))}
consider that the data sample the union of low-dimensional manifolds ${\cal M}$ isometrically embedded in ${\cal R}^O$ and introduce the manifold dimension as regularizer, namely:
\[ \min_{{\bf w}, {\cal M}} \; {\cal L}({\bf w}) + \frac{\lambda}{vol({\cal M})} \int_{\cal M} d {\bf p} \, dim({\cal M}({\bf p})), \mbox{ with } {\bf f}_{\bf w}({\bf i}_n) \in {\cal M}  \subset {\cal R}^O\]
